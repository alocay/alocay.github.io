A — Full-Bleed Hero Splash

The homepage is the statement. Giant typographic hero, minimal nav sits quietly above. Pages enter with a bold curtain/wipe transition. Each page has its own layout but shares the palette.

Pros

  • Maximum visual impact
  • Name + identity front and center

Cons

  • Hero-only pages can feel content-light

B — Split-Screen Nav (my recommendation)

Homepage splits: your name/identity on the left, navigation as large bold clickable rows on the right. Art row is visually de-emphasized. Clicking a row triggers a full-page transition. The nav is the homepage.

Pros

  • Unique and interactive homepage
  • Art de-emphasis built into the layout
  • Instantly scannable

Cons

  • Less content visible on first load

C — Editorial Grid

Homepage is a bold magazine-style grid of blocks — name, brief bio, experience teaser, contact. More content-forward, less dramatic. Inner pages still use bold typography and amber accents.

Pros

  • More information on first load
  • Easier to extend later

Cons

  • Less distinctive than A or B
